Over time, every outdoor surface around your home collects dirt, algae, mould, and grime. Driveways go grey, paths turn green, and timber decks lose their colour. Pressure cleaning (also called high-pressure washing) is one of the fastest ways to restore these surfaces and improve your home's overall appearance. But not everything should be blasted with high-pressure water. Here is a practical guide for Geelong homeowners.

## What can you pressure clean?

### Concrete driveways and paths

Concrete is one of the most satisfying surfaces to pressure clean. Years of tyre marks, oil stains, dirt, and algae come off to reveal the original colour underneath. Most Geelong driveways benefit from a clean at least once a year.

### Pavers and brick paving

Pavers around patios, pool areas, and garden paths accumulate moss and algae, especially in shaded or south-facing areas. Pressure cleaning removes the buildup and can make old pavers look almost new. Be mindful of the jointing sand between pavers — aggressive pressure can dislodge it, so you may need to re-sand afterwards.

### Timber decks and outdoor furniture

Timber decks can be pressure cleaned, but **you need to use a lower pressure setting**. Too much pressure damages the wood grain and splinters the surface. A fan nozzle at a moderate distance works best. Once clean and dry, it is a good idea to re-oil or re-stain the timber for protection.

### Fences (timber, Colorbond, and brick)

Fences cop a lot of weathering. Timber fences develop mildew, Colorbond panels collect dust and cobwebs, and brick fences grow lichen. All respond well to a careful pressure clean.

### Exterior walls and render

Rendered walls and brick exteriors can be cleaned to remove cobwebs, dust, and mould. Use lower pressure on painted surfaces to avoid stripping the paint.

### Retaining walls

Garden retaining walls, whether timber sleeper, concrete block, or stone, build up dirt and algae over time. A pressure clean restores their appearance and can highlight any structural issues that were hidden under grime.

## What should you NOT pressure clean?

Some surfaces are too delicate or can be damaged:

- **Roof tiles** — High pressure can crack tiles and strip protective coatings. Roof cleaning requires specialist equipment and soft-wash techniques.
- **Old or crumbling mortar** — Pressure can blast out mortar between bricks in older homes.
- **Single-glazed windows** — The pressure can crack glass or force water past seals.
- **Electrical fittings and outdoor power points** — Water and electricity are a dangerous combination.
- **Cars and delicate paintwork** — Use a garden hose or dedicated car wash equipment instead.

## How often should you pressure clean?

Here is a general guide for Geelong homes:

| Surface | Recommended frequency |
|---|---|
| Concrete driveway | Every 12 months |
| Pavers and paths | Every 12-18 months |
| Timber deck | Every 12 months (before re-oiling) |
| Fences | Every 1-2 years |
| Exterior walls | Every 2-3 years |
| Retaining walls | As needed |

Homes in shadier areas or properties surrounded by trees may need more frequent cleaning due to faster algae and mould growth. Properties near the coast (Torquay, Barwon Heads) also accumulate salt residue that benefits from regular washing.

## DIY pressure cleaning vs professional service

### When DIY makes sense

If you already own a pressure washer and have small jobs — a single path, a section of fence, or outdoor furniture — doing it yourself is straightforward. Consumer-grade pressure washers from hardware stores (around 1,500-2,000 PSI) handle light to moderate cleaning tasks.

**Tips for DIY pressure cleaning:**

- Always wear closed-toe shoes and safety glasses
- Start with a wide fan nozzle and test on an inconspicuous area first
- Keep the nozzle at least 300mm from the surface
- Work in consistent, overlapping passes to avoid striping
- Never point the nozzle at people, pets, or plants at close range

### When to call a professional

Professional-grade equipment operates at much higher pressure (3,000-5,000 PSI) and flow rates, which means faster results and better cleaning on tough stains. Consider a professional service when:

- **The area is large** — a full driveway, multiple paths, and a deck can take an entire weekend with a consumer machine
- **The surface is heavily stained** — oil stains, deep-set mould, and years of buildup need commercial-grade pressure
- **You need to clean at height** — exterior walls and second-storey areas are dangerous to reach with a pressure wand
- **You want to avoid damage** — professionals know the right pressure, nozzle, and distance for each surface type
- **You do not own a pressure washer** — hiring a machine, buying detergent, and spending a full day often costs more than booking a professional

## Does pressure cleaning use a lot of water?

Less than you might think. A professional pressure washer uses around 8-15 litres per minute, compared to a standard garden hose at 20-30 litres per minute. Because the water is delivered at high pressure, it cleans more effectively with less volume. A typical driveway clean uses roughly 200-400 litres — about the same as two to three loads of washing.

## Can pressure cleaning remove oil stains?

Fresh oil stains respond well to pressure cleaning, especially when a degreasing agent is applied beforehand. Old, deeply absorbed stains may lighten significantly but might not disappear completely. For best results, treat oil spills as soon as they happen.

## Maintaining results after cleaning

Once your surfaces are clean, a few habits help them stay that way longer:

- **Sweep paths and driveways regularly** to prevent dirt buildup
- **Trim back overhanging plants** that create shade and trap moisture
- **Clean up leaf litter** before it breaks down and stains concrete
- **Consider a sealer** for concrete driveways — it adds a protective layer against stains and moisture
