Geelong summers are no joke. Temperatures regularly hit the mid-to-high 30s, hot northerly winds dry everything out in hours, and water restrictions can limit when and how you irrigate. The good news is that a bit of preparation in late spring and early summer goes a long way toward keeping your garden healthy through the heat. Here is how to get your Geelong garden summer-ready.

## Mulch everything

If you do only one thing to prepare for summer, make it mulching. A 50-75mm layer of quality mulch across your garden beds is the single most effective way to protect your plants from heat stress.

**Why mulching matters in summer:**

- Reduces soil moisture evaporation by up to 70 percent
- Keeps root zones significantly cooler than bare soil
- Suppresses summer weeds that compete for water
- Prevents soil from crusting and becoming water-repellent

For most Geelong gardens, composted hardwood mulch or bark chips work well. Apply mulch after a thorough watering so you are locking moisture into the soil, not keeping it out.

### Key mulching tips for summer

- Keep mulch 50-100mm away from plant stems to prevent collar rot
- Do not mulch over dry, water-repellent soil — wet it thoroughly first or apply a soil wetting agent
- Top up any beds where existing mulch has thinned below 40mm

## Set up smart watering habits

How you water matters more than how much you water. Summer watering in Geelong should follow a few key principles.

### Water deeply and less often

Shallow daily watering encourages surface roots that are vulnerable to heat. Instead, water deeply two to three times per week. This pushes roots deeper into the soil where moisture is more stable.

### Water in the early morning

Watering between 6am and 9am minimises evaporation loss and gives foliage time to dry before evening, which reduces fungal issues. Avoid watering in the heat of the day — much of it evaporates before reaching the roots.

### Check your local water restrictions

Barwon Water manages Geelong's water supply and may impose restrictions during dry periods. Check their website for current schedules. Drip irrigation systems and hand-held hoses are often permitted even when sprinkler use is restricted.

### Consider a soil wetting agent

Geelong's sandy coastal soils and some clay soils can become hydrophobic (water-repellent) after prolonged dry spells. A granular or liquid wetting agent applied in late spring ensures water actually penetrates the soil rather than running off the surface.

## Prepare your lawn for the heat

Your lawn is the largest planted area in most Geelong gardens, and it takes the biggest hit in summer.

### Raise the mowing height

Set your mower to 40-50mm rather than the typical 30mm. Taller grass shades the soil, retains more moisture, and develops deeper roots. It also handles heat stress better than closely cropped turf.

### Mow before the worst heat arrives

Try to keep your lawn in good shape heading into December. A healthy lawn going into summer recovers much better than one that is already stressed from neglect.

### Fertilise carefully

If you missed a spring feed, a light application of a slow-release fertiliser in early December can help. Avoid fertilising once temperatures consistently exceed 30 degrees — it can burn the grass and push growth the lawn cannot sustain without extra water.

### Accept some browning

Even well-maintained Geelong lawns may go slightly dormant and brown off during extreme heat. This is normal for warm-season grasses like kikuyu and couch. They will green up again once temperatures moderate and rain returns in autumn.

## Tidy up and remove green waste

A thorough garden tidy before summer has several benefits:

- **Remove dead or dying plants** that will only look worse in the heat
- **Cut back overgrown shrubs** to improve airflow around your garden beds — good air circulation reduces pest and disease problems
- **Clear debris and leaf litter** from garden beds, paths, and gutters
- **Pull weeds** before they set seed and spread through summer
- **Prune any branches** that are shading out plants that need full sun

A clean, well-organised garden is also easier to maintain through the busy summer months.

## Choose heat-tolerant plants

If you are adding new plants to your garden, summer preparation starts with smart plant selection. Plants that thrive in Geelong's hot, dry summers include:

- **Native grasses** like Lomandra and Dianella — tough, low water, and virtually maintenance-free
- **Westringia** — an excellent hedging plant that handles heat and coastal exposure
- **Grevillea varieties** — attract native birds and tolerate dry conditions
- **Lavender** — loves heat, well-drained soil, and full sun
- **Rosemary and other Mediterranean herbs** — thrive in Geelong's summer conditions
- **Agapanthus** — hardy, drought-tolerant once established, and common in Geelong gardens for good reason

Avoid planting anything new during the peak of summer (January to February) unless you can commit to daily watering while it establishes.

## Protect vulnerable plants

Some plants in your garden may need extra help during extreme heat events:

- **Temporary shade cloth** over young or delicate plants on days above 38 degrees
- **Extra watering** for recently planted specimens that have not yet established deep roots
- **Grouping potted plants together** in a shaded area — they dry out much faster than in-ground plants
- **Moving hanging baskets** to morning-sun-only positions

## Create a summer maintenance routine

Once your garden is prepared, a simple weekly routine keeps everything on track:

1. **Monday:** Check soil moisture in key garden beds
2. **Wednesday:** Water deeply if no rain has fallen
3. **Saturday:** Mow the lawn (if needed) and do a quick weed check
4. **Ongoing:** Monitor for pests, dead-head spent flowers, and water container plants as needed
