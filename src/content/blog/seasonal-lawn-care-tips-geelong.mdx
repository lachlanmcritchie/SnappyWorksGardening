Geelong's climate sits in a unique sweet spot — warm, dry summers and cool, wet winters with plenty of wind off Corio Bay. That means your lawn needs a different approach depending on the time of year. Here is a practical, season-by-season guide to keeping your grass looking its best no matter what the weather throws at it.

## Spring (September to November)

Spring is when your lawn wakes up. Soil temperatures start rising, rainfall picks up, and grass enters its main growth phase.

### What to focus on in spring

- **Start mowing regularly.** As growth accelerates, aim to mow weekly. Set your mower height to around 30-40mm for most common Geelong lawn types like kikuyu and couch.
- **Apply a slow-release fertiliser.** Early to mid-October is ideal. Choose a balanced fertiliser to promote strong root development and even colour.
- **Tackle weeds early.** Bindii, clover, and dandelions appear in spring. Spot-treat or hand-pull them before they set seed.
- **Aerate compacted areas.** If your lawn gets heavy foot traffic, spring aeration helps water and nutrients reach the root zone.
- **Edge your lawn.** Clean edges along paths and garden beds make a huge visual difference and prevent grass from creeping into beds.

### Geelong-specific tip

Spring in Geelong can swing between warm sunny days and cold southerly winds. Hold off on any major lawn renovation (like top-dressing or overseeding) until soil temperatures consistently sit above 18 degrees Celsius.

## Summer (December to February)

Geelong summers regularly push past 35 degrees, and hot northerly winds can stress even established lawns. Water management is the priority.

### What to focus on in summer

- **Raise your mowing height.** Let your grass grow a little taller — around 40-50mm — to shade the soil and retain moisture.
- **Water deeply but infrequently.** Two to three deep soakings per week is far better than daily light watering. Water early in the morning to reduce evaporation.
- **Avoid fertilising in extreme heat.** If you missed your spring feed, wait until temperatures moderate in late February or early March.
- **Stay on top of edges.** Kikuyu runners can go wild in summer warmth. Regular edging keeps things tidy.
- **Mow in the cooler parts of the day.** Late afternoon mowing is less stressful for both you and your lawn.

### Geelong-specific tip

Geelong's water restrictions can apply during dry summers. Check with Barwon Water for current watering schedules. If restrictions are tight, focus your watering on the most visible areas and let less-used sections go semi-dormant — they will bounce back in autumn.

## Autumn (March to May)

Autumn is arguably the most important season for lawn care in Geelong. The cooler temperatures and returning rain create ideal growing conditions.

### What to focus on in autumn

- **Fertilise again.** An autumn feed with a fertiliser higher in potassium helps build root strength before winter.
- **Overseed any bare patches.** Soil temperatures in March and April are perfect for seed germination.
- **Reduce mowing frequency.** As growth slows, you can move to fortnightly mowing by late May.
- **Remove fallen leaves promptly.** Letting leaves sit on the lawn blocks light and encourages fungal issues.
- **Dethatch if needed.** If your lawn feels spongy, a light dethatch in early autumn helps water penetrate the soil.

### Geelong-specific tip

Autumn in Geelong often brings the first reliable rain after a dry summer. Take advantage of it — this is the best time for lawn renovation work because the soil is still warm but moisture is returning.

## Winter (June to August)

Growth slows dramatically in winter, especially for warm-season grasses like kikuyu and couch. The focus shifts to maintenance and preparation.

### What to focus on in winter

- **Mow only as needed.** You may only need to mow every three to four weeks, or not at all in the coldest months.
- **Keep off wet lawns.** Walking on waterlogged grass compacts the soil and damages the turf.
- **Watch for moss and fungal problems.** Shaded areas are prone to moss in Geelong's wet winters. Improve drainage and airflow where possible.
- **Plan for spring.** Winter is a good time to assess your lawn, note problem areas, and plan any renovation work for September.
- **Clean and service your mower.** Sharpen blades, change oil, and check the air filter so you are ready for spring.

### Geelong-specific tip

Geelong winters are milder than Melbourne's inland suburbs but can be persistently damp. If your lawn sits in a low-lying area, consider improving drainage during winter to avoid waterlogging issues later.

## How often should you mow in Geelong?

As a general guide for Geelong lawns:

- **Spring:** Weekly
- **Summer:** Every 7-10 days (or weekly if irrigated)
- **Autumn:** Every 10-14 days
- **Winter:** Every 3-4 weeks, or as needed
